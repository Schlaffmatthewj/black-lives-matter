Brief History From Slavery Through Jim Crow to Mass Incarceration

Prior to the 1900's

  • Slavery
  • 1513- First French and Spanish ships reached the "main-land" Americas in Florida. These ships always traveled with slaves.
  • 1619- First records showing slaves being owned in Virginia.
  • 1808- US Congress recognized the prohibition of the African slave trade. However by this point only 5% of those trades landed in the "New World"
  • 1838- Frederick Douglass escaped slavery and with this freedom he was able to publish many things as an abolitionist.
  • 1850- The Cotton Boom created vast wealth for slave owners in the South and in the North.
  • 1860- Over 75% of the world's cotton came from slave plantations in the South alone.
  • Civil War Began 1861
  • Emancipation Proclamation 1863
  • Reconstruction Era 1865
  • '65- End of Civil War
  • '65- 13th Amendment- Abolished and Prohibited all forms of slavery (expect unless you commit a crime)
  • '65 (Juneteenth)- The last known group of slaves were freed by the Union Army in Galveston.
  • Birth of KKK 1865
  • Civil Rights Act of 1866- first US federal law to define citizenship. Designed to further protect former slaves and African descendants
  • 1867- At this point over 10% of all Reconstruction Era Black politicians had faced violence from the KKK. At least were 7 politicians were killed.
  • 1868- 14th Amendment- Gave citizenship to all born or naturalized in America.
  • Jim Crow Era Begins 1870
  • '70-'71- Virginia and Tennessee pass the first "Jim Crow laws".
  • '70- 15th Amendment- Prohibited all states from refusing any citizen the right to vote based on race or color.
  • Naturalization Act of 1870- This allowed Africans and former slaves to become citizens.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1871- Prohibited race-based violence against African Americans.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1875- Prohibited discrimination in "public accommodations". (found to be unconstitutional in 1883 by Congress)
  • 1877- End of Reconstruction Era, due to the rise of Jim Crow laws and KKK violence.
  • 1982-1951- During this time period over 3,500 African Americans were lynched, 42 in 1982 alone.
  • 1892- This year saw the most documented lynchings than any other with 161.
  • 1896- Plessy v. Ferguson; "separate but equal" Doctrine- This gave States the constitutional justification for segregation. This ended the first civil rights movement time period.

1900's - Current

  • NAACP(National Association for the Advancement of Colored People) was founded in 1909.
  • The Great Migration Begins 1910-1920
  • World War 1 1914-1918
  • 1915- "the Birth of a Nation" was produced. Known today as the most controversial film ever made in the US.
  • 1919- Red Summer- At least 25 race riots occurred across the country accounted for more than 250 African American being killed by white mobs.
  • '19- 19th Amendment- This prohibited all States and Federal Government from denying the right to vote, based on Sex.
  • The Harlem Renaissance Began 1920-1935
  • '20- The KKK is strong as ever after "the Birth of a Nation", now with over 4 million members throughout the nation.
  • 1921- Tulsa, Oklahoma- Otherwise known as Black Wall Street was torched and bombed over a two-day period.
  • 1923- Rosewood, Florida- Population of 200 predominantly Black population was razed by a group of over 500 KKK members. Only 8 deaths were documented, however it is suspected to be around 150.
  • 1936- Jesse Owens wins four Gold medals in Berlin at the Olympics.
  • CORE(the Congress of Racial Equality) was Founded 1942
  • World War 2 1939-1945
  • 1944- George Stinney age 15, was executed by electric chair for an unfair trial about the murder of two white girls. 70 years later a court ruled it an unfair trial.
  • 1947- Jackie Robinson broke the color barrier playing in the Major League of Baseball.
  • 1948- Executive Order 9981- The Desegregation of the Armed Forces.
  • 1950- Ralph Bunche becomes the first African American is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his role in the Palestinian conflict.
  • '50- Gwendolyn Brooks become the first African American to be awarded the Pulitzer Prize for "Annie Allen".
  • 1953- Malcolm X becomes a minister in the Nation of Islam.
  • The Modern Civil Rights Movement Begins 1954
  • '54- Brown v. Board of Education- The Supreme Court ruled that the "separate but equal" Doctrine was unconstitutional.
  • 1955- Emmett Till, a 14-year-old boy was beat to death for allegedly flirting with a white woman. The open casket funeral shocked the world.
  • '55- Rosa Parks was arrested for not giving up her segregated bus seat in Montgomery, Alabama.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1957- This followed the ruling in Brown v. Board of Education, allowing the Government to better grasp how far segregation went in America.
  • 1957- Daisy Bates was chosen by the NAACP to be one of the "Little Rock Nine" to desegregate schools in the South.
  • SNNC(Student Non-Violent Coordinating Committee) was Founded in 1960
  • Civil Rights Act of 1960- Establishing a federal inspection of local voter registration polls.
  • 1963- Martin Luther King Jr. led a march across the Birmingham bridge.
  • '63- MLK Jr. gave the esteemed "I have a dream" speech in Washington, DC.
  • '63- The 16th street Baptist Church Bombing that killed four Black school girls, ages 11-14.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1964- Prohibited discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, and national origin by federal and state governments.
  • 1965- Malcolm X was assassinated while taking the stage to address the OAAU (Organization of Afro-American Unity).
  • The Black Panthers were Founded in 1966
  • Voting Rights Act of 1965- Prohibited any racial discrimination in voting.
  • 1967-Loving v. Virginia- The Supreme Court ruled that an earlier court's decision banning interracial marriage violated the 14th Amendment.
  • 1968- MLK Jr. was assassinated inside in motel room.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1968- Prohibited discrimination in sales, rental and financing of housing based on race, creed, and national origin.
  • Mass Incarceration Begins 1970
  • 1971- President Nixon launched the "War on Drugs" damning the Nations African American and poverty populations.
  • Comprehensive Crime Control Act of 1984- Signed by President Reagan, this set mandatory minimum sentences further damning the Nation.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1990- Vetoed by President George H. W. Bush would have made easier in court for victims of civil rights discrimination cases.
  • 1990- The KKK still exists and has over 10,000 active members.
  • Civil Rights Act of 1991- Provided the right to trial by jury on discrimination claims.
  • '91- Rodney King survived a gruesome scene that was recorded by a witness from their balcony. This was one of the first Global broadcasts of police brutality against an individual. The Video clearly showed King laying face down on the ground before any officers reached him. After 3 acquittals and a failure to reach a verdict, Los Angeles erupted in protests and riots, 63 people died in the six-day span. Eventually a federal district court found two officers guilty, who were sentenced to prison. King was awarded $3.8 million in a civil case against the city.
  • '91- Latasha Harlins 15, was shot and killed by a store owner who insisted Harlins was trying to steal a bottle of orange juice. Two witnesses contradicted every aspect of the suspect's statement. Harlins was shot in the back of her head with money in her hand. The found the suspect guilty and recommended 15 years, in which the judge determined five years probation and $500 restitution was enough. This decision many believe contributed to the fuel of the King riots. Family eventually was awarded $300,000.
  • 1994- John Ehrlichman admits that the "War on Drugs" pressed by the Nixon and Reagan Presidencies were tools for arresting hippies who were antiwar, and arresting African Americans who were simply just Black.

The Birth of the #

One Ends

Trayvon Benjamin Martin was a 17-year old African-American who lived in Florida. Trayvon was gunned down By George Zimmerman who was a member of the community watch. Trayvon was walking back from a convenience store to where his father's fiancee's home. Zimmerman called the police reporting suspicious activity by Trayvon, and the Police to Zimmerman to stand down and do not proceed. Zimmerman continued to follow Trayvon until an altercation took place that ended with Trayvon being shot in the chest and dying of his wounds. Zimmerman was not immediately arrested or charged. This saddened the Nation and people from every corner of this Country had an opinion about what had taken place. Zimmerman did eventually face charges of second-degree murder and manslaughter. Unfortunately the Florida jury acquitted Zimmerman of all charges in the case. The now sad Nation became enraged by this verdict, which lead to groups being formed for social justice.

One Begins

Alicia Garza did not fall into this situation by chance, she was raised in a mixed-race and mixed-religion household. Throughout her years in High school she was an activist for promoting school sex education about birth control. Then while attending the University of California, San Diego she was had a active role in the student association. As a senior at the university she helped organize the first Women of Color Conference in 2002, while graduating with a degree in anthropology and sociology. Following the acquittal of Zimmerman in 2013, Garza along with Opal Tometi and Patrisse Cullors took to social media to express how heartbroken they were. One of Garza first posts regarding this case read "I continue to be surprised at how little Black lives matter... Our lives matter." This post was shared by Cullors along with the #BlackLivesMatter. This was the first usage of the hashtag and the start of a movement they could only dream of.

There was clear evidence which reporters pieced together, not detectives. Ernest Satterwhite 68, Feb. 9th '14, Columbia, SC. Killed by officer Justin Craven who plead down to a misconduct of office and avoided jail time. Family settled with police department $1.2 million.

Dontre Hamilton 31, Apr. 30th '14, Milwaukee, WI. Killed by officer Christopher Manney who fired but never charged with a crime. Dontre had a history of mental illness, treated for schizophrenia. Protesting led to all Milwaukee officers wearing body cameras. Family was awarded $2.3 million.

Eric Garner 44, Jul. 17th '14, Staten Island, NY. Killed by officer Daniel Pantaleo and officer Justin D'Amico who were never indicted. Pantaleo was fired in '19. Family was awarded $5.9 million. Garner was on video saying "I can't breathe" seven times.

Levar Jones, Sep. 4th '14, Charleston, SC. Levar was shot and injured by officer Sean Groubert during a seat belt violation. Jones was reaching for his wallet from outside of his vehicle, while being shot. Jones is heard saying "What did I do, sir? Why did you shoot me?"

Vonderitt D. Myers III 18, Oct. 8th '14, St Louis, MO. Killed by off duty officer Jason Flanery who fired 17 times at Myers, hitting him 8 times mainly in the back of the legs and one the Myers head. Laquan Mcdonald 17, Oct. 20th '14, Chicago, IL. Murdered by officer Jason Van Dyke while walking away being shot in the back. The shooting was originally considered justified, 13 months later when they released the dash cam footage. That same day Van Dyke was charged with first-degree murder. Tamir Rice 12, Nov. 22nd '14, Cleveland, OH. Killed by officer Timothy Loehmann, shooting him twice, while he was playing at a playground with a toy gun. Rumain Brisbon 34, Dec. 2nd '14, Phoenix, AZ. Killed by officer Mark Rine who shot Brisbon twice in the back. Witness accounts vastly differ from Rine's police report. No charges were filed against Rine. The family was awarded $1.5 million which the City Council members did not want to pay.

Antonio Martin 18, Dec. 23rd '14, Berkeley, MO. Killed by officer Andrew Weusthoff who shot three times, striking Martin only once and his own police cruiser once. Charly 'Africa' Leundeu Keunang 43, Mar. 1st '15, Los Angeles, CA. Killed by officer Francisco Martinez shooting him three times while being pinned down by several officers on the scene. Tony Robinson Jr. 19, Mar. 6th '15, Madison, WI. Killed by officer Matt Kenny, shot 7 times while halfway down a flight of stairs. This was a call for a "wellness check" because of his history with mental illnesses. Kenny did not face any charges. Family was awarded $3.35 million.

Naeschylus Carter-Vinzant 37, Mar. 6th '15, Aurora, CO. Killed by officer Paul Jerothe who shot Vinzant within 10 seconds of arriving on the scene, who was unarmed. Grand jury did not indict him with any charges. Family was awarded $2.6 million.

Walter Scott 50, Apr. 4th '15, Charleston, SC. Murdered by officer Michael Slager who fired eight shots at Scott hitting him five times. Freddie Carlos Gray Jr. 25, Apr. 12th '15, Baltimore, MD. Died seven days after the arrest. He had slipped into a coma while being transported in a police cruiser from a severe neck injury. Sandra Bland 28, Jul. 13th '15, Hempstead, TX. Sandra committed suicide three days after the arrest, while in a jail cell. Full and Partial Bans on Choke holds, Duty to Intervene, Disciplinary Reporting, No-Knock Warrants, Re-funneling Funds In the wake of George Floyd's death and subsequent protest across the nation, dozens of cities and a handful of states have updated or even completely changed their policy regarding the use of choke holds and the responsibilities of an arresting officer. Smaller cities have seen changes regarding a "duty" to intervene and report excessive force by fellow officers, punishable by criminal prosecution. For too long police discipline records have been kept secret, even after an officer is removed from an office, the next station may not be aware of the reasons for the removal. New York has unburdened themselves of one such law, with Massachusetts and some cities following suit. Louisville, KY following Breonna Taylor's killing has voted to ban all no-knock warrants. Many cities have began shifting money from their police department into other institutions that have proven to be a great alternative to calling the police for non-criminal and non-violent calls. New York City has also proposed a $1 billion funding cut to their departments.
