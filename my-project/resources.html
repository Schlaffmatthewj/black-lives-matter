Great Black Authors & Literature

  • "The New Jim Crow" by Michelle Alexander
  • "White Fragility: Why It's So Hard for White People to Talk About Racism" by Robin DiAngelo
  • "How to Be Antiracist" by Ibram X. Kendi
  • "The Bluest Eye" by Toni Morrison
  • "The Color of Law: A Forgotten History of How Our Government Segregated America" by Richard Rothstein
  • "I'm Still Here: Black Dignity in a World Made for Whiteness" by Austin Channing Brown
  • "Queenie" by Candice Carty-Williams
  • "Between the World and Me" by Ta-Nehisi Coates
  • "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings" by Maya Angelou
  • "Well-Read Black Girl" by Glory Edim
  • "My Bondage and My Freedom" by Frederick Douglass
  • Literary Hub
  • Call Number
  • Book Depository
  • The Oprah Magazine
  • The Literature Network: Linked to Frederick Douglass

These are some of the book that top the lists. Please use these links to find more, such as children's books. These five links are a great start to educate yourself while supporting black authors as well. The Literary Hub link is amazing for not only black authors, but black owned independent book stores across the nation. The Literature Network allows you to read many archived essays and published works from great authors. Not every book I listed is authored by black people, however they are extremely relevant.
